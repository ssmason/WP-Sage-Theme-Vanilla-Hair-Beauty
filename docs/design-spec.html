Terracotta is the only accent. Used for CTAs, hover states, italic display text, and active nav. Everything else is neutral.

Colour usage rules
Backgrounds — layered warmth

Page base is Cream. Cards and the nav use White. Alternating sections use Warm. The dark footer strip uses Dark (#1C1712) for drama. One section — the CTA band — uses Terracotta as its background.

Text hierarchy
Headlines, primary body — Dark
Secondary body, descriptions — Mid
Labels, captions, meta — Stone
Eyebrows, links, accents — Terracotta
What to avoid

No pure black (#000) — use Dark instead. No blue, no purple, no green. No more than one terracotta element per viewport. Borders use Sand at 40% opacity — never bold lines. White text only on Dark or Terracotta backgrounds.

Font pairing rationale
Why Cormorant + DM Sans

Cormorant Garamond is high-fashion editorial — its fine strokes and elegant proportions signal quality and femininity without being fussy. DM Sans is clean, geometric, and slightly warm — it grounds the design and stays readable at small sizes. The contrast between them is the design. No system fonts, no generic choices.

Content tips for each team card
What to include — and what to leave out
✓
First name only — keeps it personal and approachable. Full names optional.
✓
Role title + 1–2 sentence bio — what they specialise in, written warmly. Not a CV.
✓
Speciality tags — 2–3 service tags so clients can immediately match the right person to their need.
✓
"Book with me" button — links directly to booking with that specific person pre-selected if your booking system supports it.
✕
Avoid listing every qualification — clients don't read them. One strong credibility line is enough.
✕
Avoid studio headshots with plain backgrounds — natural, in-salon photos with the team's personality showing always convert better.
